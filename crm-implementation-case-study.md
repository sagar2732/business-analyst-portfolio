CRM Implementation – Business Analysis Case Study
* Project Overview

Pitman Training Group, a long-established education provider, experienced rapid growth in online course enrollments.
Customer information was being managed using spreadsheets and manual processes, leading to inefficiencies in lead tracking, marketing follow-ups, and performance reporting.

The organization decided to implement a centralized Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system to improve operational efficiency and customer engagement.

* Business Problem

Customer data stored across multiple spreadsheets

Lack of structured sales pipeline

Poor visibility of marketing campaign performance

Ineffective follow-up on prospective leads

Limited collaboration between sales and marketing teams

These challenges resulted in reduced lead conversion and inefficient decision-making.

* Project Objectives

Implement centralized CRM platform

Automate marketing communication workflows

Improve lead management lifecycle

Enable real-time business performance dashboards

Enhance customer experience and retention

* Stakeholders

Programme Sponsor

Business Analyst Team

Project Manager

Marketing Department

Sales Department

CRM System Users

* Solution Evaluation

Three CRM platforms were evaluated:

Salesforce

ActiveCampaign

MailChimp

After cost-benefit and capability analysis, ActiveCampaign was selected due to strong automation features and lower implementation cost.

* Key Functional Requirements

Customer profile management with role-based access

Lead capture and pipeline tracking

Automated email marketing workflows

Task reminders and follow-up notifications

Performance dashboards and reporting analytics
